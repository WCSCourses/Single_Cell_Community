SinCell LATAM Community Guidelines

Our community thrives because we bring together people with different backgrounds, experiences, and perspectives. To keep this a place where everyone feels welcome, we commit to treating each other with respect.

Everyone should be able to take part in our events and platforms without worrying about discrimination or harassment of any kind—whether based on appearance, gender, identity, orientation, ability, age, race, religion, or origin.

How we show up

These behaviors help make the SinCell LATAM community a constructive and supportive space:

  • Treating each other with courtesy and respect
  • Listening to and valuing different viewpoints and experiences
  • Being upfront about conflicts of interest
  • Accepting constructive feedback with openness
  • Using inclusive and welcoming language
  • Respecting confidentiality when it is requested
  • Honoring people’s personal boundaries
  • Being mindful of power, privilege, and how much space you are taking up

What doesn’t belong here

Certain behaviors harm the community and will not be accepted:

  • Discrimination
  • Harassment of any kind (including sexual harassment)
  • Violence or threats of violence
  • Disrespectful, demeaning, or intimidating behavior

Enforcement

Breaches of these guidelines may result in moderation actions, including warnings, temporary suspension, or removal from the community, at the discretion of the steering group.

These guidelines exist to protect the community and support open, respectful scientific exchange. If you have concerns, please contact the community organizers.
